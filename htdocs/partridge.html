REVISED AT 09.50 ON FRI 10TH

 

 

SEQ 203A. INT. CARAVAN (DAY)

STUDIO.

ALAN & SONIA EATING BREAKFAST

 

ALAN:

We have lift off (EATS).

That Sonia was the best English breakfast I’ve had since Gary Wilmott’s wedding.

 

SONIA:

It was ruddy superb?

 

ALAN:

Exactly. I’d eat that 3 times a day if I could.  But I’d be (SIGHS) dead.

 

SONIA:

It kill you?

 

ALAN:

Yes, it’s cholesterol.  It’s what Scottish people eat.  Few of them make 60.  Why are there holes in my Daily Mail?

 

SONIA:

I made a collage, look.  (HOLD UP BOOK)  I cut out U2’s heads and used them to put on top of space clothes.

 

ALAN:

So the idea is that U2 are going into space.  It’s an interesting idea.  You should send that to the fan club.

 

SONIA:

I already have.

 

ALAN:

You’re not just sexy.  You’re a very good fan club member.

 

SONIA:

I also write to say my boyfriend is very big friends with Bono.

 

ALAN:

Yeah…..

 

HOLDS UP DAILY MAIL

 

ALAN:

Sonia, you appear to have inadvertently decapitated Nigel Dempster.

 

SONIA:

I can put in journal I am very good cook of full English breakfast?

 

ALAN:

Ah, I’ve made a few notes about that.  Bacon ten out of ten, button mushrooms, bingo, black pudding, snap, hash browns (American voice) [clicks fingers] platinum.

 

SONIA:

What is that?

 

ALAN:

It’s good, it’s good, it’s good.  Minor criticism.  I like to keep a bit more distance between the egg and the beans.  I may want to mix them but that’s my decision.  Use the sausage as a breakwater.  But that’s nitpicking, on the whole a very good effort.  Seven out of ten, let’s make love.

 

SONIA:

Ok.

 

ALAN:

I always find a fried breakfast makes an excellent [WIND] aphrodisiac… scuse me.

 

SONIA

we don’t wash dishes?

 

ALAN

Uh uh.  Let’s do it, right here, right now.

 

[THEY START SHIFTING FURNITURE]

 

ALAN:

Just flip that catch down, it goes the other way, no fold that bit back.  Cos I’m gonna humpyah.

[HE MOVES PLATE & WIPES IT CLEAN WITH BREAD)

Just move that.  Like deputy dawg would humpyah.  No, look at where my hands are pointing.  Find it’s housing. you should feel it click, that’s it in .  Right, shall we get a fitted sheet or just lay a big beach towel on it?

 

[SONIA DROPS WHAT SHE IS DOING AND HOLDS OUT MORE LONDON TAT]

 

 SONIA:

 

 

Alan, I buy you another thing.  It is London love taxi, I have pop my heart in back of cab and asked them to go to Alan’s stomach.

 

ALAN:

Aaaah, don’t know what you’re talking about.

Great, I’ll put that in the mini-cupboard with all the others.  (PUTS AWAY IN CUPBOARD)  It’s a bit crowded in here, like London, which I spell s-h-i-t-h-o-l-e, shithole.  C’mon, shall we start with a bit of petting?

[KNOCK ON CARAVAN DOOR – LYNN’S OUSIDE]

 

LYNN:

Alan, Alan, it’s me.

 

ALAN:

Get in Lynne.

(LYNNE ENTERS)

Hello Lynn, what’s Sonia’s best feature?

 

LYNN:

Her hair?

 

ALAN:

Try again.

 

LYNN:

Her neck?

 

ALAN:

Nope.

 

LYNN:

Her eyelashes, her eyes, all her make-up?

 

ALAN:

This is going to take forever.

It’s her feet. Come in.   She’s got lovely dainty feet. I don’t like big feet, they make me think of gammon.  Anyway, sorry it’s a mess, Sonia and I were going to make out on the day bed.

 

LYNN:

Do you want me to go and sit in the car?

 

ALAN:

No.  Not today.  Am I doing anything this evening?

 

LYNN:

No

 

 

ALAN:

(TO SONIA)Great, we’ll do it then.

(LYNNE AGREES)

(TO LYNNE)  Ok Lynne, hit me… with info.

 

LYNN:

Have you decided what you’re going to call the house?

 

ALAN:

Yes, I’ve narrowed it down to “Lord House”, “Ace House” or “The Cinammons”.

 

SONIA:

How about “Our House”?

 

ALAN:

We could do, write that down Lynne.  (GESTURES THAT SHE SCRUBS IT)

 

LYNN:

Now Alan I need to know you’re completely ready for the Inland Revenue.  Have you got all your receipts?

 

ALAN:

Yeah.  Some are on that giant spike over there.

Some are in a fat envelope you say you’ve got, and the rest are in a shoe-box I threw off a ferry.  That was the turning point.

 

LYNN:

Now be prepared, because these people can stay indefinitely.

 

ALAN:

Lynne, if my roger with Sonia gets put back again, I’ll be fit to burst.  I’ll have to resort to plan B.

 

LYNN:

Now have you got all you need?

 

ALAN:

What?  Oh, I see, yes, you are going to be here aren’t you?

 

LYNN:

Well, they’re not investigating me.

 

ALAN:

Woah.  You and I are in this together Lynne.

 

LYNN:

Well what have we got to hide?

ALAN:

Nothing.  Right, Bill Oddie gave me a dressing gown for Christmas.  He enclosed the receipt in case I wanted to take it back.  I’ve used Oddie’s receipt to claim the gown as a business expense.  I’m guilty as hell.  And I’ll tell you something Lynne, if I’m going down, you’re going down with me.  Don’t back off Lynne.  You’re in a small static home, there are only 3 ways out, there, there and there.  I feel like a stewardess.  Adopt the crash position.  Head between your legs.  No, that’s for later.  Anyway I want you to lie for me.

 

LYNN:

I can’t do that Alan, in the eyes of God…

 

ALAN:

I’m on god’s side.  I don’t like the devil, he’s bang out of order.  Evil, evil idiot.  I know the Ten Commandments say, “thou shalt not lie”, but imagine the Elephant Man came in here and put some lipstick on and a dress and said; “do I look nice”?  what would you say? 

Bearing in mind he’s depressed and got respiratory problems.  Which have driven him to experiment with hosiery.  Not full blown stockings and suspenders, something a bit more dignified.  A camisole.  What would you say Lynne?  Would you say:  “get that blusher off, you look like an ugly, misshapen-headed elephant tranny”?

 

LYNN:

No, I’d say “you look very nice.”

 

ALAN:

Exactly, you look very nice, John…

 

LYNN:

Well the tax people are going to be here in an hour.  You’ve got an hour to get ready.

 

ALAN

Yeah,

 

(TO SONIA)  What are you drawing now?

 

SONIA:

It’s an alien judge.

 

ALAN:

It’s an alien judge, golly.

 

SONIA:

 

 

 

 

And the alien judge is shooting tax man and tax man’s head is come off and all blood is squirt out.

[SHE LAUGHS AT HER OWN DRAWING]

 

LYNN:

Alan, you promised me Sonia wasn’t going to be here when the tax people came.

 

ALAN:

Well, I’ve gone and ruddy forgotten, haven’t I?

 

LYNN:

What can…

 

ALAN:

Speak quicker, she can’t pick up on this.

 

LYNN:

[QUICKLY]    What can we do about her?

 

ALAN:

[QUICKLY]  That’s not bad.  Why don’t I send her to Norwich on an errand?

[TO SONIA]  What are your plans for the day?

 

SONIA:

I go to coffee shop.

 

ALAN:[QUICKLY TO LYNNE]

Problem solved.

 

LYNN: (QUICKLY)

Pardon?

 

ALAN:

Problem solved.

 

LYNN:

(STILL QUICKLY)  Okay, well, I’ll give Sonia a lift to work.

 

ALAN:

You can speak normally now.

(TO SONIA)  Lynne’s going to give you a lift to work now.

 

SONIA:

Ok.  (STANDS)

 

ALAN:

 

 

Right, I’ve decided on the name for the house.  Excalabur Cottage.  I’m not going messing about with this one.  I want it on a polished oval slice of tree in monk letters.  Get it on your way back.

 

LYNN:

Alan, are you going to get ready for the tax people?

 

ALAN:

Chill out babe, love, lynn.  The last one.   See ya, wouldn’t wanna…oh, she’s gone.

 

ALAN MESSES ABOUT IN THE CARAVAN BY HIMSELF, TIDYING UP ETC.

 

HE GOES OPENS THE CARAVAN DOOR & LOOKS OUTSIDE AT A BUILDER…
